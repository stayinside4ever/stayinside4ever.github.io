Bookworm

While I am not exactly sure of how I acquired this reputation (quite possibly by having 40 unread books in my room at any time and almost always carrying about 5 around), a bunch of lecturers know me as the book kid at this point. Sam will occasionally check what I am reading and we might have a little discussion about it, other students will ask me if my current read is any good…

My reading tastes also vary quite greatly - for example, this week I am reading Hemingway (Islands in the Stream and The Old Man and the Sea, to be exact), Musicology by Joseph Kerman, essays on software engineering (The Mythical Man-Month), a bunch of graphic novels (Pyongyang and Shenzhen by Guy Delisle), Ancient Greek plays (Medea, The Phoenician Women, and Bacchae by Euripedes). Thankfully, people find it very easy to respect.

Would I complain if this were how people were going to remember me? Definitely not.

Also, I have come to the conclusion that walks through the forest and unexplored parts of the city when it’s sunny outside is an entire treat, especially when the day has been grand so far in general.
